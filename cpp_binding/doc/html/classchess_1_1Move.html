Detailed Description

A move that can be made of a chessboard. Move represents a half turn – one player's move.

Construct a new move from a source position, destination position, and promotion information.

Parameters
[src]src is the source square of the move (where the piece starts).
[dst]dst is the destination square of the move (where the piece ends).
[is_promote]is_promote should be true if the move is a pawn promotion.
[promote_piece]promote_piece is the type of piece the pawn is being promoted to. If is_promote is false, promote_piece is ignored.
[board]board is the board that the move will be later made on. This does not make the move – it simply needs to know what board the move will be made on so it can encode extra information (capturing, castling, etc) in the returned Move.

◆ toString()

std::string chess::Move::toString ( ) const

Convert the move to a string in pure algebraic notation.

Pure algebraic notation is of the form: <src><dst><promote?>

For example: e2e4 – moving a piece from square e2 to e4 a7a8q – moving a pawn from a7 to a8 and promoting to a queen

Captures don't have a special notation. En passant doesn't have a special notation.
